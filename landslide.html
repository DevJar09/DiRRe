Landslides

Disaster Preparedness
Emergency Procedures

Before a landslide/debris flow:

1) Check out FLASH Hurricane Strong for do-it-yourself ways to strengthen your homes against flooding, high winds and lightning.

2) Review your family preparedness plan.

3) Establish a family communications plan.

4) Assemble a disaster supply kit.

5) Have a family evacuation plan in place.

6) Perform a ground assessment of your property, and consult a professional for advice on appropriate preventive measures.

7) Learn the history of landslides and debris flows in your area. Never build a home where landslides and debris flows have occurred in the past. They are likely to occur again.

Minimize hazards

1) Plant ground cover on slopes. Use erosion-preventing plants such as yucca, bamboo, vetiver grass, etc.

2) If possible, build retaining walls, channels or deflection walls around your home.

Be familiar with landslide and debris flow warning signs

1) New cracks appear in plaster, tile, brick, foundations, sidewalks, etc.

2) Doors and windows stick or jam for the first time.

3) Outside walls, walkways or stairs pull away from the building.

4) Underground utility lines break.

5) Water breaks through the ground surface in new locations.

6) A faint rumbling sound is noticeable and increases in volume.

7) The ground slopes downward and may begin to shift.

8) Fences, utility poles, decks, floors, retaining walls and trees tilt or move.

During a landslide/debris flow:

1) Be prepared to evacuate and do so immediately if necessary.

2) If you are inside, remain where you are and take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture.

3) If you are outside, run to the nearest high ground away from the landslide or debris flow.

4) If escape is not possible, curl into a tight ball to protect your head.

After a landslide/debris flow:

1) Stay away from the slide area. There could be additional slides.

2) Remember that flooding may occur after a landslide or debris flow.

3) Help injured or trapped people. Do not move seriously injured people unless they are in immediate danger of death or further injury. Call for help.

4) Leave the house if you smell gas or chemical fumes.

4) Check for damage to walls, the foundation, the electrical system and water lines.
