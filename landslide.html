Disaster Preparedness
Emergency Procedures

Before a landslide/debris flow:

1) Check out FLASH Hurricane Strong for do-it-yourself ways to strengthen your homes against flooding, high winds and lightning.

2) Review your family preparedness plan.

3) Establish a family communications plan.

4) Assemble a disaster supply kit.

5) Have a family evacuation plan in place.

6) Perform a ground assessment of your property, and consult a professional for advice on appropriate preventive measures.

7) Learn the history of landslides and debris flows in your area. Never build a home where landslides and debris flows have occurred in the past. They are likely to occur again.

Minimize hazards

1) Know how to safely operate a fire extinguisher

2) Remember to GET OUT, STAY OUT and CALL 9-1-1 or your local emergency phone number.

3) Yell "Fire!" several times and go outside right away. If you live in a building with elevators, use the stairs. Leave all your things where they are and save yourself.

4) If closed doors or handles are warm or smoke blocks your primary escape route, use your second way out. Never open doors that are warm to the touch.

5) If you must escape through smoke, get low and go under the smoke to your exit. Close doors behind you.

6) If smoke, heat or flames block your exit routes, stay in the room with doors closed. Place a wet towel under the door and call the fire department or 9-1-1. Open a window and wave a brightly coloured cloth or flashlight to signal for help.

7) Once you are outside, go to your meeting place and then send one person to call the fire department. If you cannot get to your meeting place, follow your family emergency communication plan.
